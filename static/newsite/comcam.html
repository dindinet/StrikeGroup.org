Strike Group has two contracts with the Marine Corps to support Marine Corps Combat Camera (COMCAM) and Public Affairs (PA) units in the continental U.S., overseas and in Afghanistan. Strike Group provides support for still/video, Tactical Imagery Production Systems and Xerox high capacity printers/duplicating systems.
 
 
 
Strike Group provides; Logistics, Serialized Inventory Management, Trend Analysis, Warranty Repair Management, Preventive Maintenance, Intermediate Training, New Equipment Training, Document Control and Customer Service to support Marine Corps Systems Command, 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th Marine Divisions (MARDIV), 1st, 2nd, 3rd, Marine Air Wings (MAW), I,II,II, Marine Expeditionary Forces (MEF), Marine Logistics Groups (MLG), I,II,III Marine Corps Forces Pacific (MARFORPAC) and Reserves (MARFORRESS).
 
 
 
Marine Corps Systems Command (MCSC) Contract

MCSC Code PG11 procures and issues state-of-the-art still/video Camera Systems, and Tactical Imagery Production Systems (TIPS) to each major subordinate command and designated COMCAM/PA units world-wide in support of Global War On Terror and other operations.

COMCAM/PA camera systems are used to acquire digital imagery of important real world operations, and essential training conducted by the United States Marine Corps, Joint Forces and allies. Imagery is archived in Department of Defense (DoD) repositories for tactical, historical and operational use.

TIPS in Camp Leatherneck		 Strike Group was awarded a four year contract by the Marine Corps 1st Marine Division on 14 May 2011 to provide sustainment logistics support for three TIPS in Afghanistan that includes combat camera still/video systems and Xerox high capacity printers/duplicators. Four Field Support Representatives (FSR), were hired to provide full-time technical support in Afghanistan.










Strike Group boasts a close relationship with Xerox by working as a team to support Marine Corps Combat Camera units in Camp Leatherneck. Xerox trained FSRs provide the best support available to maintain equipment to full operating capability to support operations in Afghanistan.









TIPS contain high capacity Xerox printers/duplicators that are used extensively by units. FSRs are Xerox trained technicians responsible for performing all tasks required to maintain equipment to a high level of operation/production. FSRs conduct PM, repairs, spares management, consumables inventory/storage, tracking, accountability, equipment inventory and equipment usage reports.




TIPS also contain digital still/video and graphics multi-media production stations and assigned hand held still/video camera systems that require the same full service as on the MCSC contract. FSRs work closely with FSRs in CONUS to ensure all equipment is shipped and repaired in the most expedient manner.
